Visualising the world's billionaires

This visualisation delves into the break-down of the world's top 1% in 2023.

Wealth distribution

This map presents a vivid visual representation of the distribution of billionaires across the globe. By shading each country proportionally to its number of billionaires, patterns of wealth concentration become evident. Notably, there are striking disparities in the distribution. The USA and China, account for more than half of the billionaires. Much of the rest of the world remains lightly shaded, revealing a far lower density of such extreme wealth.

Bernard Arnault is the richest man in the world. He oversees the LVMH empire of 75 fashion and cosmetics brands, including Louis Vuitton and Sephora. He earns over $ 300,000 a day.

Elon Musk dropped from first place from 2022. He cofounded six companies, including electric car maker Tesla, rocket producer SpaceX and tunneling startup Boring Company.

Mukesh Ambani chairs and runs $110 billion (revenue) Reliance Industries, which has interests in petrochemicals, oil and gas, telecom and retail. He is the only Asian in the top 10.

Francoise Bettencourt Meyers, the granddaughter of the founder of L'Oreal, is the richest woman in the world. She inherited her fortune from the grandparents and parents.

Gender Gap

It is evident that men constitute a much larger portion of global billionaires than women. It is also important to note that most people amass their wealth much later in life.
